\section{Framework Positioning}

The Dynamic Agentic Mesh is not the only framework addressing AI-driven organisational transformation. This appendix positions it against five alternatives that the target audience may already be evaluating. The comparison is offered in good faith: each framework addresses genuine needs, and the right choice depends on organisational context, maturity, and strategic intent.

\section{Detailed Assessment}

\subsection{SAFe 6.0}

The Scaled Agile Framework added AI-specific practices in its 2024 release, including guidance on incorporating AI into agile release trains and program increment planning. SAFe's strength is its proven track record at enterprise scale: thousands of organisations have implemented it, and a large ecosystem of certified practitioners, trainers, and tooling supports deployment.

The weakness for the purposes of this report's argument is that SAFe treats AI as an augmentation to existing agile processes rather than a structural transformation. The framework does not address the coordination collapse thesis: that the information routing function of hierarchy is being automated. SAFe's release train engineers perform a coordination function that the Dynamic Agentic Mesh argues should be decomposed into automated orchestration and human judgment brokerage.

\subsection{Microsoft Copilot Studio}

Microsoft's approach integrates AI copilots directly into the productivity suite that most knowledge workers already use. The strength is adoption friction: there is no new platform to deploy, no new tools to learn, and no new infrastructure to build. For organisations already in the Microsoft ecosystem, Copilot Studio provides the fastest path to AI augmentation.

The limitation is scope. Copilot Studio augments individual productivity within existing workflows. It does not address the structural question of how organisations coordinate when AI handles information routing. The mesh concept --- with its emphasis on cross-functional orchestration, formal governance, and the judgment broker role --- addresses a different layer of the problem.

\subsection{McKinsey Agentic Organisation}

McKinsey's framework provides the strategic credibility and C-Suite access that a boutique consultancy cannot match. Their five-pillar model (business model, operating model, governance, workforce/culture, technology/data) is comprehensive at the strategic level. The limitation, as noted in Chapter~\ref{ch:industry-landscape}, is that McKinsey describes the destination without providing the architectural blueprint. The Agentic Mesh is complementary: it could serve as the operational architecture within McKinsey's strategic framework.

\subsection{Holacracy 5.0}

Holacracy's distributed authority model addresses a genuine need --- the elimination of hierarchical bottlenecks --- through a different mechanism than the Agentic Mesh. Holacracy distributes authority to self-governing circles with constitutionally defined roles; the mesh distributes information routing to agents while concentrating judgment at frontier intersection points.

Holacracy 5.0 addressed many of the scale criticisms levelled at earlier versions, but it remains culturally demanding and has no AI-specific features. For organisations attracted to distributed authority but needing AI integration, the Agentic Mesh may offer a more technology-native path.

\section{Choosing the Right Framework}

\begin{keyinsight}[title={Selection Guidance}]
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Already in SAFe}: Consider integrating mesh concepts into existing PI planning --- particularly the judgment broker role and the four KPIs. SAFe and the mesh are not incompatible.
  \item \textbf{Microsoft-native}: Start with Copilot Studio for individual augmentation; layer mesh governance concepts on top as agentic deployment matures.
  \item \textbf{Greenfield or transforming}: The Agentic Mesh provides the most comprehensive model but requires the most organisational commitment and technical expertise.
  \item \textbf{Seeking cultural transformation}: Holacracy addresses power distribution directly; the mesh addresses information routing. They solve different problems.
  \item \textbf{C-Suite buy-in needed first}: McKinsey's strategic framework provides the executive-level framing; the mesh can serve as the implementation architecture beneath it.
\end{itemize}
\end{keyinsight}
