# NYC Use Groups (1–18)

The Zoning Resolution organizes all land uses into 18 Use Groups. Each zoning district permits specific use groups, controlling what activities can occur on a lot.

## Use Group Summary

### Residential Uses

**Use Group 1 — Single-Family Detached**
- One-family detached residences
- Permitted in: R1, R2

**Use Group 2 — All Residential**
- One- and two-family residences (attached and detached)
- Multi-family residences
- All types of dwellings
- Permitted in: All R districts, most C districts (C1–C6)
- NOT permitted in: C7, C8, M1–M3

### Community Facility Uses

**Use Group 3 — Community Facilities (general)**
- Schools (public and private)
- Houses of worship (churches, synagogues, mosques)
- Libraries, museums
- Hospitals, nursing homes
- Government offices
- Non-profit offices
- Permitted in: All R districts, all C districts, M1

**Use Group 4 — Community Facilities (larger scale)**
- Colleges, universities
- Large hospitals
- Courthouses
- Correctional facilities
- Large houses of worship (2,500+ seats — special permit in some districts)
- Permitted in: R3–R10, C1–C8, M1–M3

### Commercial Uses (Local Retail & Service)

**Use Group 5 — Local Retail**
- Grocery stores, bakeries
- Drug stores, stationery stores
- Hardware stores (small)
- Florists, gift shops
- Permitted in: C1+, C2+, C3+, C4+, C5+, C6+, M1

**Use Group 6 — General Retail & Service**
- Retail stores (general)
- Eating and drinking establishments (restaurants, cafes, bars)
- Banks, loan offices
- Permitted in: C1+, C2+, C4+, C5+, C6+, C8, M1

**Use Group 7 — Home Service & Maintenance**
- Laundries, dry cleaning (drop-off)
- Tailoring, shoe repair
- Barber shops, beauty salons
- Permitted in: C1+, C2+, C4+, C5+, C6+, M1

**Use Group 8 — Professional Services**
- Medical/dental offices
- Veterinary offices
- Professional offices (small)
- Studios (art, music, dance)
- Permitted in: C1+, C2+, C4+, C5+, C6+, M1

**Use Group 9 — Local Semi-Industrial**
- Printing shops
- Plumbing/electrical supply
- Catering establishments
- Permitted in: C1+, C2+, C4+, C6+, C8, M1+

### Commercial Uses (General & Heavy)

**Use Group 10 — Large Retail & Entertainment**
- Department stores
- Theaters, cinemas
- Bowling alleys
- Permitted in: C2+, C4+, C5+, C6+, C7, C8, M1

**Use Group 11 — Hotels & Large Commercial**
- Hotels, motels
- Commercial clubs
- Tourist cabins
- Permitted in: C2+, C4+, C5+, C6+, M1 (special permit)

**Use Group 12 — General Service**
- Automobile service stations
- Car washes
- Funeral homes
- Permitted in: C2+, C4+, C6+, C8, M1+

**Use Group 13 — Automotive & Boat**
- Auto sales (new and used)
- Boat sales
- Auto repair (enclosed)
- Permitted in: C2+, C8, M1+

**Use Group 14 — Heavier Commercial**
- Lumberyards
- Building material sales
- Contractor storage
- Warehousing (limited)
- Permitted in: C8, M1+

**Use Group 15 — Amusement**
- Amusement parks
- Arenas, stadiums
- Racetracks
- Permitted in: C7, M1 (some), special permits elsewhere

### Manufacturing Uses

**Use Group 16 — Light Manufacturing**
- Bakeries (wholesale)
- Printing plants
- Woodworking shops
- Apparel manufacturing
- Electronics assembly
- Permitted in: M1+, M2+, M3, C8 (some)

**Use Group 17 — General Manufacturing**
- Heavy manufacturing processes
- Metal fabrication
- Chemical processing (limited)
- Food processing (large scale)
- Permitted in: M1+, M2+, M3

**Use Group 18 — Heavy Manufacturing**
- Cement/asphalt plants
- Junkyards, scrap metal
- Smelting, refining
- Incinerators
- Petroleum storage
- Permitted in: M3 only

## Community Facility FAR Bonus

Community facility uses (Use Groups 3–4) often have a higher FAR than residential or commercial uses in the same district:

| District | Residential FAR | Community Facility FAR |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| R6/R6A | 2.43/3.0 | 4.80 |
| R7/R7A | 3.44/4.0 | 6.50 |
| R8/R8A | 6.02/6.02 | 6.50 |
| R9/R9A | 7.52/7.52 | 10.00 |
| R10 | 10.00 | 10.00 |

This bonus applies regardless of whether the community facility use occupies the entire building or only a portion. The CF FAR applies only to the CF portion.

## Mixed-Use Buildings

Buildings can contain multiple use groups:
- Each use consumes FAR from its category (residential, commercial, community facility)
- Total floor area cannot exceed the highest single-use FAR
- Uses must be arranged vertically: manufacturing below commercial below residential (in districts that allow mixing)
- Residential and manufacturing generally cannot be in the same building (except in MX mixed-use districts)
