# Parking & Loading Requirements

## Post-City of Yes (December 2024)

The **City of Yes for Housing Opportunity** eliminated most mandatory parking minimums citywide. This is the most significant change to NYC parking policy since the 1961 Zoning Resolution.

### What Changed

**Eliminated parking mandates for:**
- All residential development citywide (all districts, all boroughs)
- Most commercial uses
- Most community facility uses

**What remains:**
- Parking **maximums** in Manhattan Core and other transit-rich areas (parking is capped, not required)
- Some parking requirements for large commercial developments outside Manhattan
- Accessible parking: ADA requirements still apply regardless of zoning
- Loading berths: still required for large buildings

### Key Principle
Parking is now **permitted but not required** in most cases. Developers can build parking if the market demands it, but zoning no longer forces minimum parking regardless of transit access.

## Pre-City of Yes Requirements (for reference and areas not yet transitioned)

These requirements applied before the City of Yes reforms and may still apply in limited circumstances during the transition period.

### Residential Parking (historical)

| District | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| R1–R2 | 1 space per dwelling unit |
| R3–R4 | 1 per DU (attached) or 1 per DU (detached) |
| R5 | 0.67 per DU (1 per 1.5 DU) |
| R6 | 0.50 per DU (1 per 2 DU) |
| R7 | 0.50 per DU |
| R8 | 0.40 per DU |
| R9 | 0.27 per DU |
| R10 | 0.20 per DU |

**Manhattan Core exemption:** Below 96th Street in Manhattan (CB 1–8), no parking was required for residential even before City of Yes.

**Transit zone exemption:** In designated transit zones (most of Manhattan, Downtown Brooklyn, LIC), requirements were already reduced to 0.50 or less per DU for affordable housing.

### Commercial Parking (historical)

| Use | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| Retail | 1 per 400 SF (outside Manhattan Core) |
| Office | 1 per 1,000 SF (outside Manhattan Core) |
| Medical office | 1 per 500 SF |
| Hotel | 1 per 2 rooms (outside Manhattan Core) |

### Community Facility Parking (historical)

| Use | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| Hospital | 1 per 8 beds |
| School | 1 per 8 classroom seats |
| House of worship | varies by district |

## Parking Maximums (still active)

In Manhattan Core and other dense areas, zoning limits how much parking can be built:

| Area | Residential Max | Commercial Max |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| Manhattan Core | 0.20 per DU | Very limited |
| Manhattan below 96th | 0.35 per DU | Case-by-case |
| Transit-rich areas | 0.50 per DU | Case-by-case |

## Accessible Parking

ADA requirements are independent of zoning and always apply:
- New multi-family buildings with 4+ units: accessible parking spaces required
- Commercial buildings: accessible spaces based on total parking provided
- 1 van-accessible space per 6 accessible spaces

## Loading Berths

Loading berth requirements have NOT been eliminated by City of Yes. They still apply based on building size and use:

### Commercial Loading
| Floor Area | Berths Required |
|-----------|----------------|
| 0–8,000 SF | 0 |
| 8,001–25,000 SF | 1 |
| 25,001–40,000 SF | 2 |
| 40,001–100,000 SF | 3 |
| Each additional 60,000 SF | +1 |

### Residential Loading
| Dwelling Units | Berths Required |
|---------------|----------------|
| 1–24 | 0 |
| 25–99 | 1 |
| 100–199 | 2 |
| 200+ | 3 |

### Berth Dimensions
- Standard: 33 ft × 12 ft × 14 ft (L × W × H clear)
- Must be accessible from the street
- Must not require backing across a sidewalk (in most districts)

## Bicycle Parking

Required for new residential and commercial buildings:

| Building Type | Requirement |
|--------------|-------------|
| Residential (10+ units) | 1 bike space per DU |
| Commercial (10,000+ SF) | 1 per 2,000 SF of office |
| Community Facility | Varies |

At least 50% of required bike spaces must be Class I (enclosed, secure storage). Remaining can be Class II (racks).

## How to Apply in Zoning Analysis

1. **Default position:** State that parking is no longer mandated for most uses (post-City of Yes)
2. **Check for maximums:** In Manhattan Core and transit-rich areas, note parking caps
3. **Loading:** Calculate required loading berths based on building area
4. **Accessible:** Note ADA requirements
5. **Bicycle:** Note bicycle parking requirements for larger buildings
6. **Caveat:** Note that City of Yes provisions are phasing in and some projects in the pipeline may still be subject to prior rules
