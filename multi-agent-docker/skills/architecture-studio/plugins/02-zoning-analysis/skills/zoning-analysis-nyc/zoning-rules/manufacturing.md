# Manufacturing Districts (M1–M3)

Manufacturing districts accommodate industrial uses, from light manufacturing and commercial to heavy industrial. They do NOT permit new residential construction (with limited exceptions in M1 districts with special permits or MX mixed-use zoning).

## Permitted Uses

### M1 Districts
Most permissive for non-residential uses:
- **Manufacturing:** Use Groups 16–17 (light industrial, not noxious)
- **Commercial:** Use Groups 4–14, 16 (offices, retail, hotels, theaters)
- **Community Facility:** Most community facility uses
- **Residential:** NOT permitted (except in MX mixed-use areas or with special permits)

M1 districts are common transition zones between commercial/residential areas and heavier industry.

### M2 Districts
- **Manufacturing:** Use Groups 16–17 (broader range than M1)
- **Commercial:** Use Groups 6–14, 16 (limited retail/service)
- **Community Facility:** Limited
- **Residential:** NOT permitted
- Higher performance standards than M3 (noise, vibration, emissions)

### M3 Districts
Most restrictive, for heavy industry:
- **Manufacturing:** Use Groups 16–18 (all industrial uses)
- **Commercial:** Use Groups 6–14, 16 (supporting commercial only)
- **Community Facility:** Very limited
- **Residential:** NOT permitted
- Lowest performance standards (most industrial activities allowed)

### Yards

| Parameter | M1 | M2 | M3 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Front Yard | None | None | None |
| Side Yards | None (unless adjacent to R) | None (unless adjacent to R) | None |
| Rear Yard | 20 ft | 20 ft | None |

**When adjacent to residential districts:** A 15-ft side yard and 30-ft rear yard are required along the M/R district boundary. Additionally, within 25 ft of an R district boundary, building height is limited to 23 ft.

### Lot Coverage
No maximum lot coverage in M districts (FAR and sky exposure plane are the controlling factors).

## Performance Standards

M districts impose performance standards on industrial uses to limit off-site impacts:

### M1 Districts (most restrictive)
- Noise: Must comply with residential noise levels at district boundary
- Vibration: Imperceptible at boundary
- Smoke/emissions: No visible smoke or particulate
- Odor: No detectable odor at boundary
- Glare/heat: No measurable impact at boundary

### M2 Districts (moderate)
- Same categories, slightly more permissive thresholds
- Industrial processes enclosed in buildings

### M3 Districts (least restrictive)
- Most categories still apply but with higher thresholds
- Some open-air industrial processes permitted

## Special Cases

### MX (Mixed-Use) Districts
Special mixed-use districts overlay M1 zones and allow residential use alongside light industrial. Designated on the zoning map as MX areas. Key features:
- Residential permitted as-of-right
- Residential FAR from the equivalent R district
- Manufacturing must comply with M1 performance standards
- Residential and manufacturing uses can be in the same building (with separation requirements)

### Industrial Business Zones (IBZ)
Not a zoning designation but a policy designation by the City. IBZs discourage residential rezonings in industrial areas. They affect land-use decisions but not as-of-right zoning controls.

### M1 Hotels
Hotels in M1 districts now require a City Planning Commission special permit (since 2018). This was to prevent hotel proliferation in industrial areas without community review.

## Parking and Loading

Manufacturing districts have specific parking requirements based on use:
- Manufacturing uses: 1 space per 1,000–3,000 SF depending on district
- Commercial uses: varies by use group
- See `parking.md` for current requirements

Loading berths required for most manufacturing buildings over 8,000 SF.
