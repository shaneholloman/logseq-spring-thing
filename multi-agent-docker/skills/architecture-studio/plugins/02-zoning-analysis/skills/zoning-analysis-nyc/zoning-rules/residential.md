# Residential Districts (R1–R10)

Residential districts are the most common zoning designation in NYC. They range from R1 (lowest density, detached single-family) to R10 (highest density, large apartment towers).

Buildings must step back above the perimeter wall height. In R1 and R2, perimeter wall is typically 21 ft, with a pitched roof allowed above.

## R6–R7: Medium-Density Residential

These districts have TWO regulatory systems:
1. **Height Factor** (standard, no suffix) — no hard height limit, sky exposure plane controls
2. **Quality Housing / Contextual** (with A/B/D/X suffix) — hard height limits, streetwall requirements

### Height Factor Regulations (R6, R7 without suffix)

| Parameter | R6 | R7 |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Base FAR (no bonus) | 0.78–2.43 | 0.87–3.44 |
| Max FAR (IH bonus) | 2.43 | 3.44 |
| Max Height | None (sky exposure) | None (sky exposure) |
| Sky Exposure Plane (wide st) | starts 60 ft, 2.7:1 | starts 60 ft, 5.6:1 |
| Sky Exposure Plane (narrow st) | starts 60 ft, 1:1 | starts 60 ft, 2.7:1 |
| Open Space Ratio | 27.5–37.5 | 15.5–25.5 |
| Front Yard (ft) | 0 | 0 |
| Rear Yard (ft) | 30 | 30 |

Open space ratio varies inversely with FAR — using more floor area requires more open space. The practical effect is that taller, slimmer towers provide more open space at grade.

## Sky Exposure Plane

The sky exposure plane is an imaginary sloping surface that limits building height at the lot's edges:

1. Building rises vertically to the **initial setback height** (varies by district and street width)
2. Above that, building must fit within a plane that slopes inward at a specified **ratio** (vertical:horizontal)
3. Wide streets (≥75 ft) have more generous planes than narrow streets (<75 ft)

The sky exposure plane encourages tower-in-the-park forms: slender towers set back from the street allow more light and air at street level.

**Tower rules (ZR 23-65):** In R6–R10 (non-contextual), a building may penetrate the sky exposure plane if:
- Tower footprint covers no more than 40% of the lot (or 50% in R9/R10)
- Tower is set back at least 15 ft from a wide street, 20 ft from a narrow street

## Rear Yard Rules

Standard rear yard is 30 ft for residential.

**Through lots** (lot fronts on two parallel streets):
- No rear yard required if lot depth < 110 ft
- If lot depth ≥ 110 ft: two 30-ft rear yards OR one 60-ft rear yard equivalent

**Corner lots:**
- Rear yard can be measured from the side street

**Rear yard modifications:**
- In R6–R10 districts on a through-lot block <230 ft deep: minimum 60 ft between facing rear walls

In R6–R10, community facility uses (schools, hospitals, houses of worship) may use a higher FAR:

| R8 | 6.02 | 6.50 |
| R9 | 7.52 | 10.00 |
| R10 | 10.00 | 10.00 |

## Inclusionary Housing FAR Bonus

In designated Inclusionary Housing areas, residential FAR can be increased in exchange for providing affordable housing:

| District | Base Res FAR | Max IH Bonus FAR |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| R6 | 2.20 | 2.43 (+10%) |
| R7 | 3.44 | 4.60 (+33%) |
| R8 | 6.02 | 7.20 (+20%) |
| R9 | 7.52 | 9.00 (+20%) |
| R10 | 10.00 | 12.00 (+20%) |

In Mandatory Inclusionary Housing (MIH) areas, the bonus is the only available FAR — you must include affordable units to develop at the stated FAR.
