# City of Yes for Housing Opportunity

Adopted by the City Council in **December 2024**, City of Yes for Housing Opportunity is the most significant zoning reform in NYC since 1961. It modifies the Zoning Resolution citywide to enable more housing production.

## Key Provisions

### 1. Universal Affordability Preference (UAP)

**What it does:** Allows a 20% FAR bonus for any residential development that includes permanently affordable housing.

**How it works:**
- Available in all zoning districts that permit residential use
- The additional floor area (up to 20% above base FAR) must be permanently affordable
- Income targets vary by area (typically 60–80% AMI)
- Available as-of-right (no special permit required)

**Example:**
- R7A district (base FAR 4.0): UAP allows up to FAR 4.8
- The additional 0.8 FAR must be 100% affordable
- On a 10,000 SF lot: 40,000 SF base + 8,000 SF affordable = 48,000 SF total

**When to apply:** Always note UAP availability in residential districts. It's the most broadly applicable City of Yes provision.

### 2. Elimination of Parking Mandates

**What it does:** Removes mandatory minimum parking requirements for residential and most other uses citywide.

**Key details:**
- Parking is now permitted but not required
- Parking maximums remain in transit-rich areas (Manhattan Core, etc.)
- Developers can still build parking where market demands it
- Accessible parking (ADA) requirements unaffected

See `parking.md` for full details.

### 3. Town Center Zoning

**What it does:** Allows ground-floor commercial uses in residential districts near transit and town centers, without requiring a commercial overlay.

**Where it applies:**
- Within designated town center areas (near transit hubs, commercial corridors)
- In residential districts R5 and above
- Limited to ground floor, small-scale retail/service (Use Groups 5–9)
- Maximum commercial space: typically 2,500 SF per establishment

**Impact on analysis:** In qualifying locations, note that ground-floor commercial may be permitted even without a C overlay on the zoning map.

### 4. Transit-Oriented Development (TOD)

**What it does:** Allows modest additional density near subway stations and major bus routes.

**How it works:**
- Properties within a specified distance of transit stations get a small FAR bonus
- Focused on low-density districts (R3–R5) where existing zoning underbuilds relative to transit access
- Allows 3–5 story buildings in areas currently zoned for 1–2 stories
- Additional density comes with affordability requirements

### 5. Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs)

**What it does:** Legalizes accessory dwelling units (garage conversions, basement apartments, backyard cottages) in low-density residential districts citywide.

**Key details:**
- Permitted in R1–R5 districts
- One ADU per lot
- Must meet building code for safety (egress, light, air)
- No additional parking required
- Homeowner-occupied building required (for garage/backyard ADUs)

### 6. Small and Shared Housing

**What it does:** Removes outdated restrictions that prevented SROs and micro-units.

**Key changes:**
- Allows studios and SRO-style units in all residential districts
- Removes minimum unit size requirements from zoning (building code minimums still apply)
- Enables shared housing models (co-living)
- Removes density factor (dwelling units per room) for buildings without unit limits

### 7. Office-to-Residential Conversion

**What it does:** Expands the geographic area and building types eligible for office-to-residential conversion.

**Key changes:**
- Previously limited to buildings built before 1977 in specific areas
- Expanded to buildings built before 1991
- Expanded geographic eligibility (more Manhattan areas, parts of Brooklyn/Queens)
- Simplified bulk rules for conversions (existing building envelope is grandfathered)

## How City of Yes Affects Zoning Analysis

When analyzing a lot, check for these City of Yes impacts:

1. **UAP bonus:** Always note the potential 20% FAR increase for affordable housing
2. **Parking:** State that parking is no longer mandated (with exceptions for maximums)
3. **Town center:** If near transit, note potential ground-floor commercial even without overlay
4. **ADU:** In R1–R5, note ADU potential
5. **TOD:** Near transit stations in low-density areas, note potential density bonus
6. **Conversion:** For existing office buildings, note conversion eligibility

## Caveats

- City of Yes was adopted December 2024 — some provisions phase in over time
- Environmental review (CEQR) may still apply to individual projects
- Community boards may comment but cannot veto as-of-right development
- Some provisions interact with special districts — check for conflicts
- The zoning text amendments are being incorporated into the ZR; some may not yet appear in online versions
- PLUTO FAR fields may not yet reflect City of Yes bonuses — calculate manually using base FAR + 20%
