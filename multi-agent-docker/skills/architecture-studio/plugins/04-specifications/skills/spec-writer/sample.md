### Section 09 30 13 — Ceramic Tiling

#### Part 1 — General

**1.01 Section Includes**

- Porcelain floor tile in lobbies, corridors, and restrooms.
- Tile base at tiled floor areas.
- Mortar setting beds, grout, and waterproof membrane at wet areas.

**1.02 Related Sections**

- Section 07 92 00 — Joint Sealants: sealant at tile-to-dissimilar-material transitions and control joints.
- Section 09 21 16 — Gypsum Board Assemblies: substrate preparation for wall tile.
- Section 22 40 00 — Plumbing Fixtures: coordination of floor drains and fixture rough-ins in tiled areas.

**1.03 References**

- ANSI A108/A118/A136.1 — American National Standard Specifications for the Installation of Ceramic Tile.
- ASTM C373 — Standard Test Methods for Moisture Absorption, Bulk Density, Apparent Porosity, and Apparent Specific Gravity of Fired Whiteware Products.
- ASTM C485 — Standard Test Method for Measuring Warpage of Ceramic Tile.
- ANSI A137.1 — American National Standard Specifications for Ceramic Tile.
- TCNA Handbook for Ceramic, Glass, and Stone Tile Installation — current edition.

**1.04 Submittals**

- Product data sheets for each tile type, setting materials, and grout.
- Samples: two full-size tiles of each type, color, and finish for Architect approval.
- Shop drawings indicating tile layout, pattern, joint widths, and transition details.
- Installer qualifications and references for projects of similar scope.
- DCOF test reports per ANSI A326.3 for floor tile.

**1.05 Quality Assurance**

- Installer shall have a minimum of five years of documented experience with porcelain tile installations of comparable scope.
- Mock-up: Provide a 4-foot by 4-foot mock-up panel installed on the actual substrate. Include tile, grout joints, and a transition to adjacent flooring. Obtain Architect approval before proceeding with production work.

**1.06 Delivery, Storage, and Handling**

- Deliver tile in manufacturer's original, unopened cartons with lot numbers visible.
- Store in a dry, enclosed area. Maintain temperature above 50 degrees F.

**1.07 Warranty**

- Manufacturer: Provide a minimum 10-year limited product warranty against manufacturing defects.
- Installer: Provide a 2-year warranty against defects in workmanship, including cracking, debonding, and grout failure.

#### Part 2 — Products

**2.01 Manufacturers**

- Daltile (a division of Mohawk Industries).
- Crossville, Inc.
- Florida Tile Industries.
- Or approved equal.

**2.02 Materials/Products**

- Porcelain tile conforming to ANSI A137.1, Group 1 (impervious), with water absorption of 0.5 percent maximum per ASTM C373.
- Tile type: through-body porcelain, rectified edges.
- Nominal size: 24 inches by 24 inches, thickness 3/8 inch minimum.
- Grout: ANSI A118.7 high-performance polymer-modified cementitious grout, non-sanded for joints 1/8 inch and narrower, sanded for joints wider than 1/8 inch.
- Setting material: ANSI A118.15 improved modified dry-set cement mortar.

**2.03 Performance Criteria**

- Dynamic Coefficient of Friction (DCOF): 0.42 minimum as tested per ANSI A326.3 (wet).
- Breaking strength: 2000 N minimum per ANSI A137.1.
- Abrasion resistance: Class IV minimum per ANSI A137.1 (public commercial traffic).
- Frost resistance: Shall comply with freeze-thaw requirements of ANSI A137.1. **[REVIEW REQUIRED]** Confirm whether frost resistance is applicable based on building envelope conditions.

**2.04 Finishes**

- Color and pattern: As selected by Architect from manufacturer's full range.
- Surface finish: Matte or natural texture. Polished finishes shall not be used in wet areas.

**2.05 Accessories**

- Tile trim: Matching porcelain bullnose or aluminum edge trim, as indicated on Drawings.
- Crack isolation membrane: ANSI A118.12 compliant, installed over concrete substrates.
- Waterproof membrane at wet areas: ANSI A118.10 load-bearing, bonded, waterproof membrane.
- Movement joint sealant: Refer to Section 07 92 00.

#### Part 3 — Execution

**3.01 Examination**

- Verify that substrates are sound, clean, dry, and free of curing compounds, sealers, and contaminants.
- Concrete substrates: Verify minimum 28-day cure. Perform calcium chloride moisture testing per ASTM F1869; moisture vapor emission rate shall not exceed 5 lbs/1000 sf/24 hours, or as recommended by setting material manufacturer.
- Verify substrate flatness tolerance of 1/4 inch in 10 feet for thin-set installations.

**3.02 Preparation**

- Scarify or grind concrete substrates that do not meet flatness tolerances.
- Apply crack isolation membrane over all concrete-to-concrete control joints and cracks.
- Lay out tile pattern from center of room or from prominent sight lines as indicated on Drawings.

**3.03 Installation**

- Install tile in accordance with TCNA Handbook methods and manufacturer's written instructions.
- Butter-back method: Apply setting material to both substrate and tile back for tiles larger than 15 inches in any dimension. Achieve minimum 95 percent mortar coverage in wet areas and 80 percent in dry areas.
- Joint width: 1/8 inch unless otherwise indicated on Drawings.
- Install movement joints at perimeter of all tiled areas, at changes in plane, and at intervals not exceeding 20 to 25 feet in each direction per TCNA EJ171.
- Grout joints after setting material has cured for minimum 24 hours.

**3.04 Quality Assurance**

- Verify 95 percent mortar coverage by periodically lifting freshly set tiles.
- Finished floor surface tolerance: 1/8 inch in 10 feet, with no lippage greater than 1/32 inch between adjacent tiles.

**3.05 Cleaning and Protection**

- Clean tile surfaces of excess grout and setting material within 24 hours of grouting.
- Apply grout sealer to all cementitious grout joints after full cure.
- Protect finished tile work with heavy kraft paper or non-staining protective covering. Maintain protection until Substantial Completion.

**3.02 Preparation**

**3.03 Installation**

- Install in accordance with manufacturer's written instructions and recommended adhesive or click-lock system.
- Stagger end joints a minimum of 6 inches between adjacent rows.
- Maintain 1/4-inch expansion gap at all fixed vertical surfaces, columns, and thresholds.
- Roll completed adhesive installations with a 100-pound roller within the time frame specified by the adhesive manufacturer.

**3.04 Quality Assurance**

- Inspect completed installation for gaps, lifting edges, pattern misalignment, and damage.
- Verify adhesion by attempting to lift a tile or plank at three random locations per 1,000 square feet.

**3.05 Cleaning and Protection**

- Clean flooring per manufacturer's instructions immediately after installation.
- Protect installed flooring with non-staining paper or breathable covering. Do not use polyethylene sheeting directly on LVT.
- Prohibit rolling loads exceeding manufacturer's published limits until project completion.

- Color-matched seam adhesive.
- Build-up strips at undermount sink cutouts for additional edge thickness.

#### Part 3 — Execution

**3.01 Examination**

- Verify that base cabinetry is installed, level, plumb, and securely anchored.
- Verify that plumbing rough-in and electrical rough-in (if applicable for under-counter equipment) are complete.
- Field-verify all dimensions prior to fabrication. Do not rely on plan dimensions alone.

**3.02 Preparation**

- Fabricate countertop sections in the shop to the maximum practical size to minimize field seams.
- Locate seams away from sink cutouts and high-stress areas. Align seam locations with Architect approval.

**3.03 Installation**

- Set countertops on a continuous bead of silicone adhesive or mechanical fasteners to base cabinets, per manufacturer's recommendations.
- Bond field seams with manufacturer's color-matched adhesive. Sand and polish seams flush with adjacent surfaces to achieve an inconspicuous joint.
- Cut sink and fixture openings with templates provided by fixture manufacturers. Ease all cutout edges and apply build-up strips as required.
- Apply sealant at countertop-to-wall junctions per Section 07 92 00.

**3.04 Quality Assurance**

- Inspect completed countertops for uniform color, level surfaces, tight seams, and absence of chips, scratches, and adhesive residue.
- Verify that seams are inconspicuous and flush.
- Verify clearances at sink and fixture cutouts.

**3.05 Cleaning and Protection**

- Clean countertop surfaces with manufacturer's recommended cleaner upon completion.
- Protect installed countertops with non-staining kraft paper or cardboard. Tape protective covering to the underside of the countertop edge; do not tape directly to the finished surface.
- Remove protection and perform final cleaning prior to Substantial Completion.
